Tusky accounts are tied to Sui wallets. There are no usernames, passwords, or email sign-ups — your wallet is your identity.

You don't need an existing Sui wallet to get started. Tusky supports **zkLogin**, which generates a self-custodial Sui address for you using a standard social login — no wallet installation required.

## Sign Up Options

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Social login (zkLogin)" icon="right-to-bracket">
    Sign in with Google, Apple, or Twitch. Tusky uses [zkLogin](https://docs.sui.io/concepts/cryptography/zklogin) to derive a self-custodial Sui address from your OAuth credential — no seed phrases, no wallet extension needed.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Sui wallet" icon="wallet">
    Connect an existing Sui wallet directly. Tusky supports [Slush](https://slush.app), [Phantom](https://phantom.app), [Suiet](https://suiet.app), and [Surf](https://surf.tech), as well as any wallet implementing the [Sui Wallet Standard](https://docs.sui.io/standards/wallet-standard).
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Create Your Account

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose your sign-in method">
    Go to [dashboard.tusky.io](https://dashboard.tusky.io) and select how you want to sign in:

    - **Social login** — click **Sign in with Google**, **Apple**, or **Twitch**. zkLogin will generate a self-custodial Sui address from your OAuth credential. You own this address — Tusky never holds your keys.
    - **Sui wallet** — click **Connect Wallet** and select your wallet (Slush, Phantom, Suiet, Surf, or any Sui Wallet Standard wallet). Approve the sign-in message in your wallet — this does not cost gas or submit a transaction.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Account provisioned">
    Once signed in, Tusky automatically provisions your account with:

    - A **prepaid wallet balance** (initially unfunded — you may add SUI and WAL in the next step)
    - A **private aggregator** (shared pool) endpoint for serving your content
    - A **managed publisher** (shared pool) for publishing to the Walrus network
    - Access to the **dashboard** for managing volumes, files, and settings
    - Two **volumes**: public and private (encrypted)
    - Access to **secrets** for storing encryption keys for your encrypted volumes
  </Step>
</Steps>
