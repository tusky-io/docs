Tusky offers a **free tier** by default and optional paid subscriptions for higher limits. All plans share the same storage model: Walrus storage costs are always paid in WAL from your [managed wallet](/core-concepts/managed-wallet) — the subscription covers only Tusky infrastructure (API, encryption, aggregators).

## How it works

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Walrus costs (WAL)" icon="database">
    Storage always costs WAL tokens, deducted from your wallet per upload. This is fixed by the Walrus network — no plan changes it.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tusky infrastructure (SUI)" icon="server">
    API calls, aggregator bandwidth, encryption, and request handling are what your subscription covers. Paid monthly in SUI.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Every Tusky account starts on the **Free** plan with no credit card required. Upgrade when you need more capacity.

## Overage behavior

When you exceed a monthly limit the behaviour depends on which limit was hit:

| Limit exceeded | Behavior |
| --- | --- |
| **API requests** | Requests return `429 Too Many Requests` until the next billing cycle or until you upgrade. |
| **Egress bandwidth** | Downloads through your aggregator are throttled to a lower rate. Files remain accessible — just slower. |
| **Environments / publishers / aggregators** | Creating new resources is blocked. Existing ones continue to work. |

<Tip>
  Watch your usage in the dashboard or via the [usage API](/api-reference/account/get-usage). You can upgrade mid-cycle at any time — the new limits apply immediately and you're charged the prorated difference in SUI.
</Tip>

## Free tier vs pay-as-you-go

The Free plan and [pay-as-you-go](/billing/pay-as-you-go) serve different use cases:

| | **Free plan** | **Pay-as-you-go (x402)** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Monthly fee | None | None |
| API requests | 1,000 / month then throttled | Unlimited, charged per request |
| Bandwidth | 5 GB / month then throttled | Unlimited, charged per GB |
| Payment timing | Prepaid (SUI in managed wallet for WAL) | Per-request at call time |
| Best for | Getting started, low-volume use | Variable traffic, cost-per-request billing |

The Free plan has hard monthly caps. Pay-as-you-go has no caps but meters every request. Both require WAL in your managed wallet for storage.

## Switching plans

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open billing settings">
    Go to **Settings → Billing** in the [Tusky dashboard](https://app.tusky.io).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select a new plan">
    Choose the plan that fits your usage. You'll see a preview of the prorated SUI charge before confirming.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Payment is deducted in SUI from your connected wallet. Your new limits apply immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Downgrading takes effect at the end of the current billing cycle. If your current usage exceeds the lower plan's limits, new resource creation is blocked until usage drops back within limits — existing data is never deleted.
