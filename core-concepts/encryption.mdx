Tusky provides **end-to-end encryption** so that private data is never exposed — not to Tusky, not to Walrus storage nodes, and not to anyone without explicit access. In v2, encryption is tightly integrated with your Sui wallet and the [Seal](https://docs.mystenlabs.com/seal) decentralized encryption protocol, removing the friction of password-based key management.

## How encryption works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Encryption seed derivation">
    When you create an encrypted drive, Tusky derives an encryption seed from your **connected Sui wallet**. This seed is the root of all encryption keys for that drive. Because the seed is tied to your wallet's cryptographic identity, only you (or members you explicitly authorize) can decrypt the data.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Client-side encryption">
    Before any file data leaves your browser or application, Tusky encrypts it locally using keys derived from the drive's encryption seed. The plaintext never reaches Tusky's servers or the Walrus network.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish encrypted blob">
    The encrypted payload is uploaded and published to Walrus like any other blob. Storage nodes only ever see ciphertext.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Decryption on retrieval">
    When you (or an authorized member) download a file, the encrypted blob is fetched from Walrus and decrypted **client-side** using keys derived from the member's wallet. The plaintext is reconstructed entirely in your browser or SDK client.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Wallet-based encryption (v2)

In Tusky v1, encryption relied on a user-chosen password. This introduced friction — forgotten passwords meant permanent data loss, and sharing required securely transmitting passwords out-of-band.

Tusky v2 eliminates passwords entirely:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Connected wallet" icon="wallet">
    Your Sui wallet's keypair is used to derive encryption seeds. Signing a deterministic message produces a stable seed without ever exposing your private key. As long as you control your wallet, you control your data.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Seal integration" icon="shield-halved">
    For shared drives, Tusky leverages **Seal** — a decentralized encryption protocol on Sui. Seal smart contracts manage access policies on-chain, enabling secure key sharing without Tusky ever holding decryption keys.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  Because encryption is now tied to your wallet instead of a password, there is nothing to remember or lose. Connect your wallet, and your encrypted drives are instantly accessible.
</Tip>

## Seal and token-gated access

[Seal](https://docs.mystenlabs.com/seal) is a decentralized encryption infrastructure built on Sui. Tusky uses Seal to power access control for encrypted drives, enabling powerful token-gated and policy-driven sharing.

### How Seal works with Tusky

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Access policies">
    Drive owners define **on-chain access policies** through Seal smart contracts. These policies specify which Sui addresses (or which NFT / token holders) can decrypt the drive's contents. Policies are transparent and verifiable on the Sui blockchain.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Key management">
    Seal distributes encryption key shares across a decentralized network of key servers. No single server holds enough information to reconstruct a key. When an authorized user requests decryption, Seal key servers verify the on-chain policy, and only then release their key shares.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Token gating">
    You can gate drive access on **any on-chain condition**: ownership of a specific NFT collection, holding a minimum token balance, membership in a DAO, or custom Move-based logic. This enables powerful use cases like subscriber-only content, DAO-gated archives, and NFT-unlockable media.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Info>
  Seal policies are composable. You can combine multiple conditions — for example, requiring both NFT ownership **and** a minimum SUI balance — to create fine-grained access rules.
</Info>

## Encrypted drive workflow

Here's the complete flow for data in an encrypted drive:

```
┌─────────────┐     ┌──────────────┐     ┌─────────────┐     ┌──────────────┐
│  Your device │────▶│ Encrypt with │────▶│  Publish to  │────▶│   Walrus     │
│  (plaintext) │     │ wallet seed  │     │   Walrus     │     │  (ciphertext)│
└─────────────┘     └──────────────┘     └─────────────┘     └──────────────┘

                          UPLOAD FLOW ──────────────────▶

◀────────────── RETRIEVAL FLOW

┌──────────────┐     ┌──────────────┐     ┌─────────────┐     ┌──────────────┐
│   Walrus     │────▶│  Fetch via   │────▶│ Decrypt with │────▶│  Your device │
│  (ciphertext)│     │  aggregator  │     │ wallet seed  │     │  (plaintext) │
└──────────────┘     └──────────────┘     └─────────────┘     └──────────────┘
```

### Key security properties

| Property | Guarantee |
|---|---|
| **Zero-knowledge** | Tusky never sees your plaintext data or holds your encryption keys. |
| **Client-side only** | Encryption and decryption happen exclusively in your browser or SDK client. |
| **Wallet-bound** | Keys are derived from your Sui wallet — no passwords to manage or leak. |
| **Decentralized key management** | Seal distributes key shares so no single point of failure can compromise access. |
| **On-chain policies** | Access rules are enforced by smart contracts, fully transparent and auditable. |

## Sharing encrypted drives

When you add a member to an encrypted drive, Tusky uses Seal to grant that member's Sui wallet address decryption access:

1. The drive's Seal policy is updated on-chain to include the new member's address.
2. When the new member requests a file, Seal key servers verify their address against the policy.
3. If authorized, the key servers release key shares, enabling client-side decryption.

The drive owner never transmits raw keys, and Tusky never acts as an intermediary for key material.

<Warning>
  Removing a member revokes their future decryption access through Seal. However, any data the member already downloaded and decrypted locally cannot be "un-shared." Treat drive membership as a trust decision.
</Warning>

## Migration from v1

If you used password-based encryption in Tusky v1, your drives will be migrated to wallet-based encryption. During migration:

1. You authenticate with your original password one final time.
2. Tusky re-encrypts your drive's key material under your connected Sui wallet.
3. The password is discarded and no longer needed.

<Note>
  After migration, your encrypted drives are accessible solely through your Sui wallet. Make sure you have secure backups of your wallet's seed phrase.
</Note>
