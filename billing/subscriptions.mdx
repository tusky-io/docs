title: Subscriptions
description: 'Fixed-rate plans with transaction and bandwidth limits'
---

Subscriptions offer an alternative to [pay-as-you-go](/billing/pay-as-you-go) billing for teams and applications that want **predictable, fixed-rate costs**. Each plan provides monthly allowances for transactions, bandwidth, and storage, paid in SUI.

## How subscriptions work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose a plan">
    Select a subscription tier that matches your expected usage from the [Tusky dashboard](https://app.tusky.io).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pay in SUI">
    Subscription fees are paid monthly in SUI. Payment is processed at the start of each billing cycle.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use your allowances">
    Each plan includes monthly limits for transactions, bandwidth, and storage. Usage is tracked in real time on the dashboard.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Walrus costs remain pay-per-use">
    The subscription covers Tusky infrastructure costs. Walrus storage costs (WAL) are still deducted from your [prepaid wallet balance](/core-concepts/prepay) on each upload.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  A subscription replaces the 10% Tusky infrastructure fee on each transaction. Instead of paying per-upload fees, your subscription covers Tusky's infrastructure costs for the month. You still need WAL in your managed wallet for Walrus storage payments.
</Info>

## Plans

Tusky offers tiered subscription plans to fit different usage levels. The following tiers are representative — visit the dashboard for current pricing and limits.

| | Starter | Growth | Scale |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Transactions / month** | 1,000 | 10,000 | 100,000 |
| **Bandwidth / month** | 10 GB | 100 GB | 1 TB |
| **Storage** | 50 GB | 500 GB | 5 TB |
| **Best for** | Side projects, prototypes | Production apps, growing teams | High-volume platforms, enterprises |

<Note>
  Plan limits and pricing are subject to change. Always check [app.tusky.io](https://app.tusky.io) for the latest plan details.
</Note>

## What's included

Each subscription covers:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Tusky infrastructure fee" icon="server">
    The 10% per-transaction fee is waived. Your subscription covers API services, encryption, and aggregator hosting.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Aggregator bandwidth" icon="globe">
    File downloads served through your private aggregator count against your monthly bandwidth allowance.
  </Card>
  <Card title="API transactions" icon="code">
    Every API call (upload, download, list, delete, etc.) counts toward your monthly transaction limit.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Storage capacity" icon="hard-drive">
    Total data stored across all vaults, measured at the Walrus blob level.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### What's NOT included

<Warning>
  **Walrus storage costs (WAL)** are not included in the subscription. You still need WAL tokens in your [managed wallet](/core-concepts/managed-wallet) to pay Walrus storage nodes for each upload. The subscription only covers the Tusky infrastructure layer.
</Warning>

## Overage behavior

When you exceed your plan's monthly limits:

| Limit | Behavior |
| --- | --- |
| **Transactions** | Additional requests may be throttled or charged at an overage rate depending on your plan. |
| **Bandwidth** | Downloads may be throttled once the monthly allowance is exhausted. |
| **Storage** | New uploads are blocked once the storage cap is reached. Existing files remain available. |

<Tip>
  Monitor your usage in the dashboard or via the [usage API endpoint](/api-reference/account/get-usage) to avoid hitting limits unexpectedly. Upgrade your plan before reaching capacity for uninterrupted service.
</Tip>

## Subscriptions vs pay-as-you-go

| Feature | Subscription | Pay-As-You-Go |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Pricing model** | Fixed monthly fee (SUI) | Per-transaction (SUI via x402) |
| **Tusky fee** | Included in subscription | 10% on each upload |
| **Walrus cost (WAL)** | Pay-per-use from prepaid wallet | Pay-per-use from prepaid wallet |
| **Cost predictability** | High — fixed monthly rate | Variable — depends on usage |
| **Transaction limits** | Monthly cap per plan tier | Unlimited (pay per request) |
| **Bandwidth limits** | Monthly cap per plan tier | Unlimited (pay per request) |
| **Best for** | Predictable workloads, production apps | Variable usage, experimentation |
| **Overage handling** | Throttled or overage charges | No limits — always charged per use |
| **Commitment** | Monthly billing cycle | None — pay only when you use |

## Best for

Subscriptions are ideal when:

- Your application has **predictable traffic patterns** and you want to budget costs in advance.
- You prefer a **fixed monthly bill** over variable per-transaction charges.
- You are running a **production application** and want to avoid per-request payment overhead.
- You want to **eliminate the 10% per-upload fee** and replace it with a flat rate.

<Info>
  You can switch between subscription and pay-as-you-go at any time. When you cancel a subscription, you revert to pay-as-you-go billing at the end of the current billing cycle.
</Info>

## Managing your subscription

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Dashboard">
    Navigate to **Settings → Billing** in the [Tusky dashboard](https://app.tusky.io) to:
    - View your current plan and usage
    - Upgrade or downgrade your plan
    - Cancel your subscription
    - View billing history
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SDK">
    ```typescript
    const account = await tusky.account.get();

    console.log("Plan:", account.plan);
    console.log("Billing cycle:", account.billingCycle);

    const usage = await tusky.account.usage();
    console.log("Transactions this month:", usage.apiRequests);
    console.log("Bandwidth this month:", usage.bandwidthBytes);
    console.log("Storage used:", usage.storageBytes);
    ```
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
